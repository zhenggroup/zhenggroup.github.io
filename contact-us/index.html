in situ analysis of electrochemical interface

Electrochemical interface is where the electron transfer between electrode/electrocatalyst and electrolyte happens. Understanding the interface reveals the structure-property relationship of electrocatalysts. In situ techniques are useful to study the interface.
Our lab focuses on the following techniques: in situ UV-Vis and Raman spectroscopy, Atomic Force Microscopy, and X-ray diffraction.

Rational material design for electrocatalysis

Electrocatalysts are materials that can promote electrochemical conversion at fix potential. For best efficiency, the materials need to have suitable surface atomic configuration and adorption energy. The best way of achieving this is rational design of active sites.
Our lab focuses on the following reactions: carbon dioxide electrochemical adsorption and reduction; electrochemical organic synthesis; water splitting.

Integrated electrocatalytic system

Electrochemical conversion is not only about the design of electrocatalysts, but the overall system. Sometimes, electrochemistry can be combained with other methods for higher efficiency, such as photoelectrocatalysis.
Our lab focuses on the following systems: 3D printed electrochemical cell and multilayer electrochemical cell.
