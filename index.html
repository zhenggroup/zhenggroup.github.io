We are the Electrochemical Interface and Conversion (EIC) Group
at Guangdong Technion (GTIIT)
广东以色列理工学院电化学界面与转化研究组

We are interested in electrochemically charged interface and *in situ* characterization techniques for electrochemical conversion processes, such as water splitting reaction and carbon dioxide reduction. We aim to understand and solve the real-life problems in electrochemical conversion from an atomic/molecular level.

Our research philosophy:

The first principle is that you must not fool yourself—and you are the easiest person to fool. So you have to be very careful about that. After you’ve not fooled yourself, it’s easy not to fool other scientists. You just have to be honest in a conventional way after that.
— Richard P. Feynman
